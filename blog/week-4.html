Today we learned about Arrays and Hashes. I've programmed in python before, so I was familar with how to store information in both of them. However, the syntax is a little different from Python, and I had to learn the differences through trial and error.

Arrays and Hashes are basically just ways to store information. Arrays pair the information to a specific number (starting from 0, then going upwards to infinity from there), and Hashes pair the information to a keyword that the user assigns. It's great if you're trying to store password information or account information.

We actually use and call arrays for special characters. Every time you press alt + (a 3 digit number) in order to make a special character, you're accessing a specific id number for an array in order for your program to return a special character (like a copywrite symbol) to you.

Hashes are more for storing information that you want to remember. Specifically, they're easier to understand than arrays because rather than having information tied to a number, it's instead tied to a word-pair. You can keep a list of personal achievements, or perhaps list your favorite greeting and your favorite goodbye. Both hashes and arrays are quite useful!
